Which user moment or touchpoint are you targeting?

What friction or missed opportunity are you solving for? (3-5 sentences)

During an overdose, every second counts. But many people don't know where to find naloxone or how to respond. Downloading an app takes too long, and stigma often prevents people from seeking help openly. Canada's opioid crisis requires safe, quick, and private access to overdose response resources, a perfect fit for Reactiv! Our app adresses these issues by enabling isntant access to nearby naloxone kits, guides, and emergency contacts through a single QR scan or NFC tap, no downloads or delays needed :)

### 2. Proposed Solution

**How is the Clip invoked?** (check all that apply)

**End-to-end user experience** (step by step):
1. User scans a QR code or taps an NFC tag located in public spaces such as transit stops or shelters where drug overdoses are common
2. The App Clip launches instantly, showing the nearest naloxone kit locations using Ontario's naloxone database'
3. THe user can view step-by-step instruction for administering naloxone, call emergency services, and access support resources directly from the Clip

**How does the 8-hour notification window factor into your strategy?**
If a user recently interacted with the App Clip, they can recieve notifications regarding tips on recovering from an overdose and further information regarding help groups and other resources

### 5. Impact Hypothesis

How does this create measurable business impact? Be specific about:
- Which channel benefits (in-person, online, or both)?
- What conversion or engagement improvement do you estimate, and why?
- Why this touchpoint is the right place to intervene

NaloxoneNow supports communities, public institutions, and transit networks by providing an easy way to improve public safety. Channels of shelters, high-traffic areas, and transit all benefit.
